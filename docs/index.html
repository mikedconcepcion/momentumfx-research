Abstract

We investigate whether supply-demand zones form preferentially during periodic "Order Block" (OB) time windows and whether this pattern persists across multiple asset classes and extreme market regimes including the COVID-19 crash. Analyzing 2,417,398 five-minute bars across five instruments (EURUSD, GBPUSD, USDJPY, XAUUSD, BTCUSD) spanning January 2020 to December 2025 (6 years), we find statistically significant concentration of zone formation during OB windows (œá¬≤ = 158.78, p < 0.001). Pattern persisted through COVID-19 crash with concentration factor of 2.80x. Gold (XAUUSD) demonstrated highest consistency with 95.3% OB concentration. Results suggest genuine institutional order flow timing patterns that traders can incorporate as a filter within complete trading systems.
